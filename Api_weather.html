Detailed Weather Impact Analysis

Heatwave Impact on Dairy Cows

When temperatures exceed 25Â°C (77Â°F), dairy cows experience heat stress, leading to reduced feed intake, decreased milk production, and compromised reproductive performance. Heat stress can reduce milk yield by up to 35% during peak summer months.

Key Precautions:

  • Install cooling systems and fans
  • Provide adequate shade structures
  • Increase water availability
  • Adjust feeding schedules to cooler hours

Winter Cold Effects on Dairy Production

Cold temperatures below 5Â°C (41Â°F) increase energy requirements for maintaining body temperature. Cows need additional calories, affecting feed conversion efficiency and potentially reducing milk fat content if nutrition isn't properly managed.

Essential Measures:

  • Provide windbreaks and shelter
  • Increase energy-rich feed
  • Ensure unfrozen water access
  • Monitor for frostbite on udders

Monsoon Challenges in Dairy Farming

Excessive rainfall creates muddy conditions, increases disease risk, and affects pasture quality. High humidity combined with moisture promotes bacterial growth, leading to mastitis and hoof problems in dairy cattle.

Prevention Strategies:

  • Improve drainage systems
  • Regular hoof trimming and care
  • Maintain dry bedding areas
  • Enhanced hygiene protocols

Extreme Weather Emergency Response

Cyclones, floods, and severe storms pose immediate threats to livestock safety and farm infrastructure. Emergency preparedness and quick response protocols are essential for minimizing losses and ensuring animal welfare.

Emergency Actions:

  • Develop evacuation plans
  • Secure emergency feed supplies
  • Install early warning systems
  • Maintain insurance coverage
